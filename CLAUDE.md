## Writing style

Do not use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) anywhere in writing or code comments.
Rewrite to avoid them rather than substituting a different dash.

Use instead:
- A comma for a slight pause: "It's clean, but it's underselling the work."
- Parentheses for an aside: "The build (Xcode and SwiftUI) is the differentiator."
- A colon to introduce or expand: "The wedge was simple: combine both apps' strengths."
- Two separate sentences for a hard break: "Most portfolios stop at a prototype. This one shipped."

Hyphens are fine only inside compound modifiers, e.g. "map-first", "no-login", "user-centred".

If any em or en dashes slip through, find and replace them before finishing.
