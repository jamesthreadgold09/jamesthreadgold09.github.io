FreshTrack

Smart food management for budget-conscious students, tackling UK food waste and financial hardship at once.

Role
Product Designer, UX Researcher, Business Analyst
Type
iOS mobile application
Context
UWE Bristol, Dissertation Project
Model
Freemium, three revenue streams

The problem

UK households waste £14 billion worth of food annually. Yet students, the demographic with the least financial resilience, are among the worst affected. Existing tools either lacked student-specific features or were built around US infrastructure. No single app combined fridge tracking, expiry management, budget-conscious meal planning and shared-accommodation coordination for UK students.

My primary research put numbers to it: 52% of students regularly overspend on groceries and 53% throw food away two or three times a week, losing roughly £273 a year to expired food alone. In shared houses of three or more it compounds, with 18% higher overspending and 17% more food expiring before it is used.

Research & insights

I approached it as both a designer and a business analyst, grounding the product in evidence before designing a single screen. That meant primary research with 103 UK students, alongside a PESTLE macro-environment analysis, a competitive landscape review, and empathy mapping of budget-stressed students.

A handful of findings shaped the product directly:

  • 47% regularly forget what food they already have, driving duplicate buying and waste. That made fridge tracking the foundation of the app.
  • The three most wanted features were budget alerts (61%), expiry reminders (58%) and recipe suggestions using what you already have (46%), so those became the three core screens.
  • 76% preferred a freemium model over paying upfront, which set the business model.
  • Simplicity was the number one success factor: the target was a maximum of 30 seconds to log an item.

The competitive gap

Mapping the market showed plenty of food apps, but none built for this user.

  • Mealime (£50/yr) and PlateJoy (£99/yr) target affluent, general audiences with US-centric content and measurements, priced well out of a student budget.
  • Generic budgeting apps treat food as just another expense category, with none of the food-specific intelligence.
  • UK tools like Cherrypick and Mealia prove grocery integration works, but aren't built around student-specific needs.

The gap was clear: no app combined fridge tracking, budget-conscious meal planning, UK retailer integration and shared-living coordination for students. That gap is FreshTrack.

The solution

A freemium iOS app that answers the three validated needs head on: a fridge tracker so you always know what you have, budget alerts that flag overspending early, and AI-powered meal suggestions for items nearing expiry. It also builds smart shopping lists and lets flatmates coordinate. Integration with Tesco and Sainsbury's APIs provided real-time pricing and automatic basket building, turning awareness into action at the moment of purchase.

90% of consumers are aware of food waste, but only 67% actively work to reduce it. FreshTrack bridges that gap.
